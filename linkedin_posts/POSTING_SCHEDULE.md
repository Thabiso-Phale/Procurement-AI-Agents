**Copy:**
How to negotiate better prices with your suppliers — without damaging the relationship:

Step 1: Come with data, not demands
Pull competitor quotes first. "I've found this at £X" is a fact. "I want a discount" is a demand. Facts win negotiations.

Step 2: Give them a reason to say yes
"If we can agree £X, we'll commit to a 12-month contract." Certainty of volume is worth a discount.

Step 3: Ask at the right moment
Best time: before renewal, not during crisis. A supplier who knows you're desperate has no incentive to budge.

Step 4: Negotiate payment terms, not just price
Net 60 instead of net 30 on a £200k/year supplier is worth £10k+ in working capital.

Step 5: Document whatever you agree
A verbal handshake means nothing when the rep changes. Get it in writing, every time.

Relationships aren't damaged by professional negotiation. They're damaged by surprises and inconsistency.
